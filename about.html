Who we are

The Zephaniah Owidi Odong(ZOO) Memorial Foundation is inspired by the life of The Late Zephaniah Owidi Odongo who:

  • Grew up in extreme poverty in rural Kenya.
  • Through education, dedication and the good will of others in supporting his determination, overcame serious adversity, excelled in his scholarship.
  • Became a distinguished Electrical Engineer who contributed significantly to the Kenyan Telecom industry and perfomed activities supported by this organization.
  • Believed and demonstrated that education really is the key out of poverty, and that technology bridges the world.

What we do

We:

  • Set up ICT centers – Beneficiaries of The ZOO Memorial Foundation have fully functional ICT centers with donated computers and internet connection.
  • Training – The foundation provides and facilitates computer literacy training to its beneficiary center trainers and members of the community.
  • Creating awareness – Societies around beneficiary centers are encouraged and enabled to take part in ICT activities such as using message boards, chat rooms and email.
  • Advocacy – Sensitising local companies and organizations on the importance and participation in digital revolution in Kenya.

Why ICT centers?

A number of converging trends make our foundation's Rural ICT Project relevant in ICT facilitation and accessibility especially in rural Kenya.

  • Increased use of ICT for both economic and social development.
  • Growth of mobile telephony and mobile service providers.
  • Increased necessity for use of ICT and limited skilled persons.
  • Limited or no ICT access to majority of rural Kenya.
  • Technology shy – we see this as an opportunity to make changes within an evolving industry.

How we do it

  • We partner with schools and community based organizations
  • Set up ICT centers by donating computers
  • We train the trainers
